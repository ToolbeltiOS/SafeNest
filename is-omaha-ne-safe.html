Crime in Omaha vs. the national average

Omaha reports 369 violent crimes per 100,000 residents, 3% below the U.S. average of 381, and 3,162 property crimes per 100k versus a national 1,954 (62% above). In the most recent FBI reporting year (2024), the violent crime rate fell 34.2% year over year, a pattern SafeNest classifies as improving.

Flood & natural hazard risk

Federal disaster records for Nebraska — the state SafeNest uses for Omaha's flood context — list 20 FEMA flood disaster declarations between 1960 and 2019, most recently "Severe Storms And Flooding". Nebraska has also seen 1 hurricane or tropical-storm declaration, events that frequently drive flooding. These are state-level federal declarations, not a parcel-level flood-zone determination — a specific Omaha address may sit inside or well outside a FEMA flood zone, which the SafeNest app checks directly by address.

Schools in Omaha

Public schools in Omaha average 4.6/10 on the SafeNest School Score across 167 rated schools. By level, high schools rate strongest at 6.6/10 on average, while middle schools average 3.5/10. The highest-scoring is Prairie Lane Elementary School at 9.8/10.

Registered offenders

Registered-offender data is meaningful only at the address level, so SafeNest does not publish a city-wide figure for Omaha: a single citywide number would blend blocks with very different profiles and mislead more than it informs. Exact counts and locations for any specific Omaha address are available in the SafeNest app, drawn directly from the state's public registry.
