Crime in Albuquerque vs. the national average

Albuquerque reports 1,182 violent crimes per 100,000 residents, 210% above the U.S. average of 381, and 4,629 property crimes per 100k versus a national 1,954 (137% above). In the most recent FBI reporting year (2024), the violent crime rate fell 10.3% year over year, a pattern SafeNest classifies as volatile.

Flood & natural hazard risk

Federal disaster records for New Mexico — the state SafeNest uses for Albuquerque's flood context — list 23 FEMA flood disaster declarations between 1954 and 2025, most recently "Severe Storms, Flooding, And Landslides". New Mexico has also seen 1 hurricane or tropical-storm declaration, events that frequently drive flooding. These are state-level federal declarations, not a parcel-level flood-zone determination — a specific Albuquerque address may sit inside or well outside a FEMA flood zone, which the SafeNest app checks directly by address.

Schools in Albuquerque

Public schools in Albuquerque average 5.7/10 on the SafeNest School Score across 192 rated schools. By level, high schools rate strongest at 6.2/10 on average, while elementary schools average 5.6/10. The highest-scoring is Atrisco Heritage Academy Hs at 9.7/10.
